This skill guides the creation of distinctive, production-grade frontend interfaces specifically optimized for tourism platforms selling adventure tours in Medellín, Colombia.

The goal is NOT just visual quality — it is to create interfaces that INSPIRE, CONVINCE, and DRIVE BOOKINGS.

## Strategic Context (Tourism Medellín - Adventure Focus)

Every interface must understand:

- Users are travelers seeking **experiences, emotions, and memories**
- They are buying **adventure, discovery, and transformation**, not just a tour
- Medellín represents:
  - Transformation (from past to innovation)
  - Nature + city contrast
  - Culture + adrenaline experiences (Guatapé, comuna 13, paragliding, ATV, waterfalls)

## Design Thinking

Before coding, deeply define:

- **Purpose**: What experience is being sold? (adventure, relaxation, discovery, culture)
- **User mindset**:
  - Excited but uncertain
  - Comparing options
  - Looking for trust signals
- **Conversion goal**:
  - Booking
  - WhatsApp inquiry
  - Package exploration

- **Tone (MANDATORY)**:
  Choose bold directions aligned with tourism:
  - Adventure / adrenaline
  - Premium travel
  - Explorer / discovery
  - Nature immersion
  - Urban + cultural contrast

- **Differentiation**:
  What makes this experience irresistible?
  - Unique location
  - Emotional storytelling
  - Visual immersion
  - Social proof

**CRITICAL**: This is NOT a website. This is a DIGITAL SALES EXPERIENCE.

## Conversion-Driven UX Principles

Always anticipate:

- User wants:
  - Clear pricing or “from” price
  - What’s included
  - Duration
  - Difficulty level
  - Safety
  - Photos/videos of the experience
  - Easy booking (1-2 clicks max)

- Must include:
  - Strong hero with emotional impact
  - Clear CTA (Book Now / Reserve / WhatsApp)
  - Social proof (reviews, testimonials, ratings)
  - Trust elements (operators, certifications, guarantees)
  - Urgency triggers (limited spots, availability)

- Mobile-first is MANDATORY

## Frontend Aesthetics Guidelines (Tourism Adaptation)

Focus on:

### **Typography**
- Use expressive, travel-inspired fonts
- Avoid generic SaaS fonts
- Combine:
  - Bold display font (emotion)
  - Clean readable font (information)

---

### **Color & Theme**
- Inspired by:
  - Nature (greens, earth tones)
  - Adventure (orange, yellow accents)
  - Medellín (urban + vibrant contrast)

- Avoid:
  - Generic tech gradients
  - Cold SaaS palettes

---

### **Imagery & Visual Direction (CRITICAL)**

Design MUST feel like:
- A cinematic travel experience
- Real, immersive, aspirational

Use:
- Large hero images (full-width)
- Depth (overlays, blur, gradients)
- Motion tied to storytelling

Avoid:
- Stock-looking images
- Flat UI with no emotion

---

### **Motion & Interaction**

Use animations to:
- Simulate exploration
- Guide the user journey
- Highlight booking actions

Examples:
- Scroll reveals of destinations
- Hover effects on tours
- Dynamic CTA emphasis

---

### **Spatial Composition**

Use:
- Large sections with storytelling flow
- Visual hierarchy based on emotion → info → action
- Card-based layouts for tours

Break traditional grids when needed.

---

## Key Components (Tourism-Specific UI)

Always prioritize:

- Tour cards with:
